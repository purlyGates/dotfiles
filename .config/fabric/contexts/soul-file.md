The sky appears blue because of a phenomenon called **Rayleigh scattering**. Sunlight, although it looks white, is actually made up of many colors—each with different wavelengths.

As sunlight passes through Earth’s atmosphere, it collides with molecules and small particles. **Blue light** has a shorter wavelength and is scattered in all directions much more than other colors (like red, which has a longer wavelength and is scattered less).

When you look up during the day, this scattered blue light reaches your eyes from all directions, making the sky appear blue.

At sunrise and sunset, the sun’s light passes through more atmosphere, so most of the blue light is scattered out of view and the sky appears red or orange instead.
